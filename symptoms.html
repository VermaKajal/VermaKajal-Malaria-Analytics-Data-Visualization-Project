Symptoms

The most common early symptoms of malaria are fever, headache, and chills. Symptoms usually start within 10–15 days of being bitten by an infected mosquito. These symptoms may be mild for some people, especially those who have had malaria before. Because some malaria symptoms are not specific, getting tested early is important. Severe symptoms include:

  • Extreme tiredness and fatigue

    Individuals with malaria often experience overwhelming fatigue, leaving them physically drained even after minimal activity.

  • Impaired consciousness

    Severe malaria can affect the brain, leading to confusion, disorientation, or even loss of consciousness, requiring immediate medical intervention.

  • Multiple convulsions

    In advanced stages, malaria may trigger repeated seizures, indicating serious neurological involvement and a critical condition.

  • Difficulty breathing

    Respiratory distress or difficulty in breathing can occur as the infection progresses, sometimes caused by fluid buildup in the lungs or organ failure.

  • Dark or bloody urine

    Hemolysis, the breakdown of red blood cells, may lead to dark or bloody urine, a symptom known as “blackwater fever” in severe malaria cases.

  • Jaundice

    Malaria can cause liver dysfunction, leading to jaundice, where the skin and eyes take on a yellow tint due to a buildup of bilirubin.
